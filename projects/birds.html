Avian Evolution in Amazonia

Amazonia harbors a huge diversity of birds, many of which are endemic there. The ornithologists involved with the Amazonia Project are all specialists in systematics and phylogeography and have been working on Amazonian birds for many years. Our goals are to build phylogenetic trees of many Amazonian bird groups at fine taxonomic scale (at the phylospecies level) and use those to investigate systematic relationships and historical biogeographic patterns. We will also be undertaking detailed phylogeographic studies of selected bird groups.


In addition to these studies the Team at the American Museum of Natural History, in collaboration with other collections in the U.S. and Brazil, is building a database of georeferenced specimens of birds and primates to support the vertebrate studies of this Project.
