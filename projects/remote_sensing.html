Remote sensing and modeling

Research in this subproject will include:

Inverse Modeling:

As a way to integrate biodiversity data and large-scale modelling of vegetation-climate interactions, we will use Earth System Models (of intermediate complexity) to simulate potential ground cover patterns according to various sets of boundary layer conditions (i.e. differences in atmospheric CO2, temperature and precipitation). Computer-generated vegetation patterns will be compared against phylogenetic trends in bird, butterfly and primate biodiversity. Closeness-of-fit will be determined as a way to potentially explain the underlying biological-climatological mechanisms promoting areas of high endemism.

Earth System Modeling:

The geological record indicates that prior to the start of the Pleistocene, there existed a vast area of water covering the Amazon Basin. Earth System models will be used to test hypotheses related to the role of this shallow (but persistent) lake in altering regional (basin) climatology, in altering the global carbon-silicon cycles, as well as determining patterns of vegetation type and structure across the Amazonian landscape.
