Phylogeny and Phylogeography of Primates

The goal of this subproject is to understand how climate and geological changes occurring in Amazonia in a period between the Miocene and Pleistocene contributed to the diversification of selected groups of New World primates and to phylogeograpic patterns of some New World primates genera such as Callicebus, Saimiri, Chiroptes, Saguinus and Mico.

The hypothesis are:

  1. The rivers from Southwestern Amazonia were determinants in the diversification of Mico genus;
  2. Saguinus diversification was due to an eastern or central corridor North-South that precedes the formation of the transcontinental Amazon (North→ Saguinus midas - South → Saguinus niger);
  3. Saimiri from South Amazon diversified first in the Western and than crossed the Amazon River to North at ~2 Mya;
  4. The diversification of Chiropotes species as well as of Callicebus from both banks (North and South) of Amazon River precedes the formation of the transcontinental
